getaway car.

With the sun setting behind us and city lights below us, The air around me grew dense as it mixed With your lust as you blew out that last puff. My breathing grew heavy as my vision got blurry Fixated on you as you stood up and leaned in closer You knew, didn't you? When our lips brushed against each other, The unfathomed distance between us floated by Fading far away from the chains of reality That broke with each stride you took towards me. You knew, didn't you? Your hands ran down my body Claiming their trophy, my body your prize My heart, your prisoner. But long before your touch, your gaze Had already corrupted its way inside my breast. But you knew, didn't you? As your tongue tasted mine It grew a liking to that warmth. That warmth fuelled by my pain and your fears Soon plummeted into a fire Now burning with your pain and my fears. But you knew, didn't you? You knew this fire wouldn't extinguish Yet you kept it burning With the same old fears, the same old tears. Only to be tamed by our agony and misery But you left, leaving me with my burning carcass Because you knew, didn't you? My burning body still covered in our debris. They told me to hate you but how could I? When the only love I had known was to hate myself I knew I pushed you away, as my unfounded wrath Became the keys to your getaway car.
